*comment ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
*comment  THE LANTERN ROAD
*comment  A short demo story for the StoryScript Engine.
*comment ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

*create name "traveller"
*create lanterns 3
*create courage 50
*create coin 10
*create has_map false
*create has_lantern true
*list inventory ["worn boots", "water flask"]
*object player {"hp": 100, "maxhp": 100}

*theme {"--bg": "#f0e6cc", "--text": "#1c1a14", "--choice-bg": "#e8dab8", "--choice-border": "#8a7145", "--accent": "#5c3d0e"}

You wake on a cold road with no memory of how you arrived here.

The lanterns lining the path ahead flicker. Somewhere in the dark, something breathes.

You reach into your coat. You have ${lanterns} lanterns left.

*page_break

*choice
	#Call out into the dark
		You cup your hands. "Hello?"
		
		The breathing stops.
		
		Then — footsteps, slow and deliberate, approaching from the tree line.
		
		A figure resolves out of the fog: an old woman in a travelling cloak, a lantern swinging from one fist.
		
		"I thought you were a ghost," she says. "You look like one."
		
		*gosub meet_elder
	#Hold perfectly still
		You press yourself against a stone wall and wait.
		
		The breathing fades. Whatever was out there moves on.
		
		After a long minute, your heartbeat settles.
		
		*set courage courage + 5
		
		You notice a folded paper on the ground nearby. A map, half-ruined by damp.
		
		*set has_map true
		*push inventory "damp map"
		
		You study it as best you can. There is a village, two miles east.
		
		*goto road_to_village
	#Run
		*set courage courage - 15
		
		You run blind, boots sliding on wet stone, until your lungs give out.
		
		When you stop, gasping, you are at a crossroads you do not recognise.
		
		*set lanterns lanterns - 1
		
		You burned through a lantern in the panic. ${lanterns} remain.
		
		*goto crossroads

*label meet_elder

The old woman — she gives her name as Maren — is matter-of-fact about everything.

"The road ahead is watched," she says. "Toll-keepers. Not the official kind."

She studies you. "You look like you've got nothing to lose. That's either brave or stupid."

*page_break

*choice
	#"I have ${coin} coin. Is that enough to pass?"
		Maren squints. "For one person, and if you don't linger, maybe."
		
		She takes one coin from you and tucks it away. "Call it advice money."
		
		*set coin coin - 1
		
		"The toll-keepers want three coin each. You've got enough — just barely. Don't spend it."
		
		*set courage courage + 10
		*goto road_to_village
	#"I'll go around them."
		"Through the Briar?" Maren looks unimpressed. "You'll lose an eye."
		
		She sighs and reaches into her pack. "Here. You'll want this."
		
		She hands you a folded map.
		
		*set has_map true
		*push inventory "Maren's map"
		
		"The Briar path adds two hours. But no toll-keepers."
		
		*goto briar_path
	#"What's in it for you?"
		Maren smiles. It does not reach her eyes.
		
		"I like to see who makes it through. Consider me curious."
		
		She gives you nothing further — but she does step aside and let you pass without trouble.
		
		*set courage courage %+ 20
		
		*goto road_to_village

*label briar_path

The Briar is exactly as unpleasant as advertised.

*gosub spend_lantern

Thorns drag at your coat. The path loops back on itself twice before straightening.

*if has_map
	Your map — such as it is — keeps you oriented. You pick the correct fork without losing much time.
*else
	Without a map you guess at every fork. You guess wrong twice. You're tired and scratched when you finally emerge.
	*set courage courage - 10
*endif

*page_break

You come out on the far side of the Briar onto a moonlit road. Ahead: lights. A village.

*goto village_gates

*label road_to_village

The road is straight and flat and watched.

Two figures stand at a makeshift gate across the road. One holds a lantern. One holds a club.

"Three coin," says the one with the club. "Each way. You look like a one-way kind of traveller, so three coin."

*page_break

*if coin >= 3
	*choice
		#Pay the toll
			*set coin coin - 3
			
			The gate swings open. The one with the lantern nods, as if you've passed some kind of test.
			
			"Smart," he says. "Some people fight us. They don't reach the village."
			
			*goto village_gates
		#[if courage >= 60] Stare them down
			You hold the toll-keeper's gaze for a long, cold moment.
			
			Something in your expression gives him pause. He looks at his partner. His partner looks at the ground.
			
			"...go on then," he mutters, and opens the gate.
			
			*set courage courage + 15
			
			*goto village_gates
		#Double back through the Briar
			You weigh your coin. You weigh the dark.
			
			You turn around.
			
			*goto briar_path
*else
	You don't have enough coin. The toll-keeper can see it on your face.
	
	"Briar or back the way you came," he says. "Your choice."
	
	*choice
		#Into the Briar
			*goto briar_path
		#Back the way you came
			*set courage courage - 10
			*goto crossroads
*endif

*label crossroads

You are at the crossroads. Three roads. No signage.

You have ${lanterns} lanterns. You have ${coin} coin.

*if has_map
	You check the map. East is the village. West leads back toward wherever you came from. North is unmarked.
*else
	You have no map. You guess.
*endif

*page_break

*choice
	#Go east
		*goto road_to_village
	#Go north
		*set lanterns lanterns - 1
		
		The north road climbs. The fog thickens. After an hour you find a ruined waystation.
		
		Inside: a locked chest, a dead fire, and scratched into the wall: TURN BACK.
		
		*set courage courage - 5
		
		*if lanterns = 0
			Your last lantern goes out in a gust through the broken roof.
			
			You sit in the dark for a very long time.
			
			Eventually you turn back, feeling your way by touch.
			
			*set courage courage - 15
		*endif
		
		*goto crossroads
	#Go west
		The west road is familiar in an unsettling way — as if you've walked it in a dream.
		
		You walk for an hour and arrive back where you started.
		
		You have learned nothing. You have ${coin} coin and ${lanterns} lanterns.
		
		*goto crossroads

*label village_gates

*theme {"--bg": "#ede0c0", "--accent": "#3d6b3a", "--choice-border": "#5a8a56"}

The village is real.

Warm light leaks under doors. Smoke rises from chimneys. A dog barks once and goes quiet.

You count your inventory as you walk the main street.

*temp item_count 0
*for item in inventory
	*set item_count item_count + 1
*endfor

You're carrying ${item_count} item(s):

*for item in inventory
	— ${item}
*endfor

*page_break

An inn sits at the end of the street, its sign a painted lantern. Inside: firelight and voices.

*choice
	#Go inside
		The innkeeper is a broad-shouldered person with a grey braid and a look of professional welcome.
		
		"Travelling alone?" they ask. "Road's been strange tonight. You're the third one through."
		
		*if coin >= 2
			*set coin coin - 2
			"A room and a meal — two coin. You look like you need both."
			
			You eat. You sleep. In the morning, the road looks possible again.
			
			*goto ending_rested
		*else
			"A room's two coin," they say. "Meal's included."
			
			You show them your purse.
			
			They look at it for a moment. "Fireplace is free. I won't charge you for the chair."
			
			*goto ending_chair
		*endif
	#Keep walking
		You don't know what you're looking for.
		
		But the village falls behind you, and the road opens up, and for the first time tonight the sky is clear.
		
		*goto ending_road

*label ending_rested

*theme {"--bg": "#e8eed8", "--accent": "#3a6632", "--choice-border": "#5a8a50"}

You sleep without dreaming, which is the best kind of sleep.

In the morning you find a note slipped under your door. No signature. Just an address, three towns east, and one word: come.

Your courage is ${courage}. Your coin is ${coin}.

You have survived the Lantern Road.

*finish

*label ending_chair

*theme {"--bg": "#e8eed8", "--accent": "#3a6632", "--choice-border": "#5a8a50"}

The fire is warm enough. The chair is harder than a bed, but you've slept in worse.

At some point in the night the innkeeper drapes a blanket over you without waking you.

Your courage is ${courage}. Your coin is ${coin}.

You have survived the Lantern Road.

*finish

*label ending_road

*theme {"--bg": "#f0e6cc", "--accent": "#5c3d0e", "--choice-border": "#8a7145"}

The road goes on. The lanterns are running low. You don't stop.

Somewhere behind you, the village light fades.

Somewhere ahead, the sky begins to pale.

Your courage is ${courage}. Your coin is ${coin}.

You have survived the Lantern Road.

*finish

*label spend_lantern
*if lanterns > 0
	*set lanterns lanterns - 1
*endif
*return
